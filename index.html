About Background & Skills

Seb Andrews is a fifth-year coterminal student at Stanford University pursuing an M.S. in Earth Systems, following a B.S. in Earth Systems. His research spans climate and energy policy, GIS and spatial analysis, extractive industry accountability, and urban equity — with a focus on how subnational governance structures shape climate outcomes.

He has worked at the Environmental Defense Fund, co-authored a peer-reviewed publication, and conducted research on local government sustainability capacity in the Bay Area. He goes professionally by Seb Andrews.

His GIS work centers on bivariate symbology, census-derived equity indicators, and 3D scene analysis — currently building a displacement and heat vulnerability dataset for California using ACS variables in ArcGIS Pro.
